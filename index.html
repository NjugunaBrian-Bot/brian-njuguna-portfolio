01

Eliminating the #1 Driver of Repeat Contacts: Account Merge Resolution

3,000 weekly tickets eliminated Read full case study +

Executive Summary

Account merge failures were the single largest driver of repeat customer contact at Branch International — a recurring hit to CSAT and FCR, week after week. I led a cross-functional investigation and fix that removed the issue at its root, taking 3,000 tickets a week out of the queue permanently.

Context & Challenge

Customers whose national ID was already linked to another account — often with a loan that was current, overdue, or in default — were getting stuck, generating repeat contacts across in-app, social, and call centre channels. The team was triaging these cases one at a time, which capped how much CSAT and FCR could improve.

Strategic Approach

  • Audited support logs to quantify account merge as the top contact driver
  • Investigated the underlying cases: existing loans current, overdue, or in default
  • Partnered directly with Product and Engineering instead of continuing symptom-level fixes
  • Built a daily handling cadence so new cases couldn't re-accumulate into a backlog

02

Authoring the 2026 Customer Experience Strategy

Retention · cost · trust — one roadmap Read full case study +

Executive Summary

CX initiatives at Branch were reactive and siloed, with no shared plan tying retention, cost, and product trust together. I authored a company-wide strategy that gave every initiative a clear owner, target, and risk — turning "we should fix this" into a working roadmap.

Context & Challenge

Leadership needed one plan spanning three goals at once: keep customers who were at risk of churning, cut the cost of every contact, and rebuild product trust after recurring service failures — without any of the three cannibalizing the others.

Strategic Approach

  • Retention: a rapid fraud-escalation framework targeting a 2-minute escalation and 24-hour resolution for fraud cases, plus a structured recovery flow for dissatisfied customers
  • Cost optimization: a Workforce Management system to cut shrinkage from 26% toward 15%, and chatbot expansion to absorb up to 50% of chat volume
  • Trust & product usage: an in-app "Know Your Score" loan-health dashboard, and an NLP sentiment-analysis proposal (BERT, DistilBERT, BERTopic) to capture unsolicited product feedback
  • Named the support needed and the real blockers for every initiative, rather than presenting a wish list

03

Running a 13-Person Team Across 7 Channels Without Dropping SLA

SLA, QA, and productivity held — at scale Read full case study +

Executive Summary

A distributed team spanning in-app, email, phone, SMS, social, and call centre across two markets needed consistent SLA, QA, and coverage — without burning people out. I rebuilt the operating rhythm around scheduling, coaching, and recognition, and response time dropped by two-thirds.

Context & Challenge

Seven channels, two markets, and 13 people meant coverage gaps were easy to miss until they showed up as SLA breaches. Leave planning, off-day swaps, and overtime tracking were inconsistent, and there was no shared view of who was strong where.

Strategic Approach

  • Rebuilt scheduling with leave planned quarters in advance and off-day swaps checked against coverage before approval
  • Standardized shift handovers and ticket routing to prevent queue buildup at shift changes
  • Ran biweekly 1:1s tied directly to QA audits, so coaching was specific, not generic
  • Introduced a weekly team scorecard and monthly rewards & recognition process, posted publicly to the team
  • Built a structured two-week onboarding path with message-volume targets so new hires ramped predictably

04

Diagnosing Low CSAT & FCR with AI-Powered Sentiment Analysis

A data-backed root-cause proposal Read full case study +

Executive Summary

CSAT and FCR were low, and manual QA review could only sample a fraction of conversations. I wrote the problem statement and technical proposal for an NLP sentiment-analysis solution to review 100% of conversations instead of a sample — and surface exactly which issues were driving dissatisfaction.

Context & Challenge

QA teams could only manually review a small slice of chats, so tone problems and recurring pain points were caught late, if at all. Branch already had the raw data — CSAT responses (Typeform), agent responses, and closed-ticket metadata — but no way to turn it into a prioritized action list.

Strategic Approach

  • Sentiment analysis (NLP): pre-trained models (BERT, DistilBERT) fine-tuned on Branch's own tone and language to classify messages as positive, neutral, or negative
  • Emotion classification: going beyond polarity to detect anger, confusion, disappointment, and gratitude
  • Topic modeling (BERTopic): clustering conversations to surface what customers were actually complaining — or praising — most
  • Correlation analysis: pairing sentiment with CSAT and FCR to find patterns, e.g. negative tone + long resolution time reliably predicting a low CSAT score

Impact

Reframed QA from manual sampling to a prioritized queue — flagging negative-sentiment conversations for review automatically, identifying root-cause issues like "loan limit" or "KYC rejection" faster, and giving leadership a live read on unsolicited product feedback. Adopted into the 2026 CX Strategy and is now in build discussions with the Engineering team.

05

Turning PIN Resets Into Self-Service — and Lifting the App Store Rating

Google Play rating: 4.2 → 4.5 stars Read full case study +

Executive Summary

PIN resets were a high-volume, fully manual request — every one needed an agent, which meant delay for the customer and repetitive load on the team. I partnered with Product and Engineering to design a self-service PIN reset flow, and the resulting drop in friction showed up directly in the app's public rating.

Context & Challenge

Locked-out customers had no way to recover access without contacting support, so PIN resets were a constant, recurring contact driver. The wait for an agent — however short — was long enough to show up in customer reviews, and it was pulling the Google Play Store rating down.

Strategic Approach

  • Flagged PIN reset as a top candidate for self-service based on its volume and how repetitive/low-risk the request pattern was
  • Worked with Product and Engineering to define the verification logic and flow so it stayed secure without needing an agent in the loop
  • Represented the support team's view of the failure points and edge cases during design and testing
  • Coordinated the rollout with the support team so agents could handle the (much smaller) volume of edge cases the self-service flow didn't cover
