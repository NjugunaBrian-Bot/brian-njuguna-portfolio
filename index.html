Case Studies Five problems I was handed, and how I closed them out. 01 Eliminating the #1 Driver of Repeat Contacts: Account Merge Resolution 3,000 weekly tickets eliminated Read full case study + Executive Summary Account merge failures were the single largest driver of repeat customer contact at Branch International — a recurring hit to CSAT and FCR, week after week. I led a cross-functional investigation and fix that removed the issue at its root, taking 3,000 tickets a week out of the queue permanently. Context & Challenge Customers whose national ID was already linked to another account — often with a loan that was current, overdue, or in default — were getting stuck, generating repeat contacts across in-app, social, and call centre channels. The team was triaging these cases one at a time, which capped how much CSAT and FCR could improve. Strategic Approach Audited support logs to quantify account merge as the top contact driver Investigated the underlying cases: existing loans current, overdue, or in default Partnered directly with Product and Engineering instead of continuing symptom-level fixes Built a daily handling cadence so new cases couldn't re-accumulate into a backlog Impact 3,000 Weekly tickets removed ↑67% CSAT (post-fix) ↑18% FCR (post-fix) Freed the team to focus capacity on harder, higher-value cases instead of a recurring, fixable failure.
